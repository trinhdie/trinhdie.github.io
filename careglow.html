Overview

Mobile first for accessibility

CareGlow is a patient-centered healthcare companion app designed to simplify how people manage their medical lives. Built-in translation ensures it supports users of all languages — book appointments, manage insurance, estimate costs — all in one place, with clarity and confidence.

"After spending 2 years in the healthcare industry, it became clear that surprise billing is patient's biggest concern — creating a breakdown of trust between patients, insurance, and providers, with no obvious point of accountability."

— Research Insight

The Problem

Healthcare friction begins with billing

Extensive survey research and interviews with real providers and patients revealed that most adults struggle most with health insurance — specifically a lack of pricing transparency. Young adults and elderly people have become particularly vulnerable, regardless of background.

🏥

Front Desk Overload

Medical assistants can't resolve billing questions — patients wait months for resolution.

📋

Insurance Opacity

Patients delay appointments over billing fears, leading to irregular checkups and reduced care quality.

🌐

Language Barriers

Non-English speakers face compounded friction in a system not designed for their needs.

Process

Sketch → Sitemap → Wire → Build

Initial ideation began with paper sketches, rapidly translating user needs into low-fidelity wireframes. The site map guided information architecture, ensuring a logical patient journey from onboarding through appointment management and billing.

Low-fidelity wireframes were tested with real users to validate core flows before moving to high-fidelity screens.

User Research

Who we're designing for

Interviews and surveys surfaced a recurring archetype: the unpaid family caregiver who coordinates care for a loved one while managing their own full life. Linda represents this primary user — and her needs drove every design decision in CareGlow.

LN
Persona 01  ·  Primary

Linda Nguyen

Age 46  ·  Suburban Southern California
Project coordinator (healthcare admin)  ·  Caring for her 78-year-old mother at home

Background & Tech Habits

  • Works full-time; comfortable with Gmail, Calendar, Notes, FaceTime, and Facebook — uses iPhone all day but not an early adopter.
  • Coordinates with siblings via group text and shared Google Sheets to track appointments and expenses — chaotic and hard to keep current.
  • Often the single point of contact for medical updates, then relays them to the family.

Goals

  • Keep her mother safe and as independent as possible at home.
  • Create a shared view of tasks, meds, and appointments so siblings can genuinely share the caregiving load.
  • Reduce mental overload — wants to "know everything's handled" without holding every detail in her head.

Frustrations & Pain Points

  • Information scattered across sticky notes, texts, patient portals, and her own memory.
  • Siblings say they want to help but rarely see exactly what needs to be done or when.
  • Feels guilty and burned out; caregiving work is invisible and unacknowledged.
  • Generic to-do apps and spreadsheets feel too manual — not built for caregiving workflows.

Behaviors

  • Checks her phone early morning and late night to plan the day and catch up on messages.
  • Attends medical appointments and then "translates" updates for the family.
  • Will adopt new tools if: onboarding is simple, the caregiving benefit is clear, and she can invite siblings easily.

Needs from CareGlow

  • Caregiver-centric task dashboard: what's due today, what's overdue, and who's responsible.
  • Simple way to log symptoms, meds, and doctor instructions right after an appointment.
  • Lightweight sibling coordination — assign tasks, send reminders, share status updates without a complicated app.
  • Emotional support through small acknowledgments: streaks, encouraging micro-feedback, and visible progress.

Behaviors in CareGlow

  • Opens Tasks first thing in the morning to see what's outstanding.
  • Assigns tasks to siblings directly from the task modal — uses Name Filter to check their progress.
  • Uses the Bill Calculator before appointments to estimate costs and reduce billing surprises.
  • Relies on Calendar sync and haptic notifications so nothing falls through the cracks.

I'm the default caregiver for everything. I just want one place that shows who's doing what so it's not all in my head.

Low Fidelity Wireframes

Paper to pixels — testing before building

Lo-fi wireframes were created for every core screen before any visual design began. Each screen was tested with real users to validate information hierarchy, flow logic, and accessibility before moving to high-fidelity.
