Overview

Approaching the Uncertainty of Fuel Sustainability

Long-distance commercial transportation — such as tour buses, park shuttles, and inter-city mobility services — remains heavily dependent on diesel and high-emission fuel systems. HydraVoyage is a product experience that reframes hydrogen mobility not just as an engineering solution, but as a design challenge: giving operators confidence and passengers clarity as the hydrogen fuel transit industry scales.

The mission: to travel more sustainably by reframing hydrogen mobility as a design for public experience — operated by commercial partners, accessible to everyday riders.

The Problem

Three barriers standing between hydrogen and everyday transit

Hydrogen fuel promises cleaner long-distance transit, but real-world adoption is blocked by infrastructure gaps, invisible sustainability benefits, and fundamental trust deficits — none of which are purely engineering problems.

01

Infrastructure Scarce & Unreliable

Hydrogen fueling stations are rare outside major corridors. Operators face route anxiety and unplanned downtime — making fleet confidence difficult to build without real-time visibility into station availability and capacity.

02

Sustainability Benefits Are Invisible

Passengers choosing hydrogen-powered transit have no way to see the actual emissions they're avoiding. Without tangible carbon metrics, the sustainability argument stays abstract — and the premium of choosing clean transit goes unrewarded.

03

A Fundamental Trust Question

Autonomous and hydrogen-powered systems feel unfamiliar to most riders. Building trust requires an interface that makes the technology legible — not hidden — so passengers feel safe, informed, and in control.

User Research

Who we're designing for

Research surfaced a clear secondary user beyond tourists and fleet managers: the independent operator who depends on a commercial vehicle for their livelihood. Jasmine represents this persona — and her scheduling and fuel-planning pressures directly shaped the operator-side feature set.

JL
Persona 01  ·  Small Business Operator

Jasmine Lopez

Age 33  ·  Los Angeles metro
Small business owner  ·  Gas cargo van · Same-day deliveries & event setups

Context & Habits

  • Runs a small events and staging business — hauls equipment, furniture, and decor between warehouses, venues, and clients.
  • Works mainly in dense urban traffic with occasional longer drives to out-of-town venues.
  • Uses calendar, maps, and route-planning apps to squeeze multiple jobs into one day.

Goals

  • Keep fuel costs predictable and manageable across the month.
  • Avoid mid-day refueling that eats into tight schedules.
  • Plan loads and routes to eliminate backtracking and idle time in traffic.

Pain Points

  • Last-minute client reschedules or venue changes force inefficient detours and unplanned fuel spend.
  • Hard to estimate how much gas a multi-stop day will actually require before starting.
  • Occasional van overloads increase fuel consumption and raise safety concerns.

Behaviors

  • Plans her day early each morning and adjusts routes on the fly via phone.
  • Tracks expenses manually in spreadsheets or bookkeeping software at night.
  • Motivated by tools that make her feel "on top of the business" — not just driving.

Design Needs

  • Multi-stop route planning that estimates total fuel usage based on cargo weight, distance, and traffic patterns.
  • Suggestions for ideal refuel windows between jobs, with ETA and buffer time shown.
  • Trip summaries showing fuel used per job or per day — to inform pricing and budgeting decisions.
  • Clear warning when a planned route plus load is pushing fuel and time beyond safe margins.

How HydraVoyage Helps

  • Station Operational Status System gives her real-time visibility into hydrogen and fuel availability along her routes.
  • Passenger Flow and route dashboards adapt directly to cargo logistics and multi-stop scheduling.
  • Predictive fuel alerts flag risk before she leaves — eliminating mid-day surprises.
  • Trip summary data feeds directly into her end-of-day expense tracking workflow.

I can't afford surprise fuel runs in the middle of a tight schedule. I need to know my day's gas needs before I even start the engine.

Design Rationale

Commercial deployment first

By starting with commercial operators — tour companies, national park shuttles, city tourism boards — long-distance commercial hydrogen transportation can enter public life gradually. These controlled, high-visibility deployments build the confidence and infrastructure that eventually unlock broader public adoption.

Multiple sketch iterations explored different navigation metaphors — from map-centric to data-dashboard layouts. The final direction emerged from testing which approach reduced cognitive load while maintaining full situational awareness for operators.

🔁

Routes are predictable and repeatable — commercial tourism runs fixed corridors, eliminating range anxiety.

⚡

High vehicle utilization maximizes environmental impact per vehicle deployed.

🏗

Centralized fueling, maintenance, and training are practical at commercial scale.

🌱

Passengers experience clean transit without changing their behavior — zero friction adoption.

Strategy

Since hydrogen fuel has limited public visibility, engaging with the expanding tourism industry provides a way to push hydrogen into everyday lifestyle — creating a foundation for broader public transit and individual mobility adoption over time.

Brand Identity

HydraVoyage — built for trust

The HydraVoyage wordmark pairs a geometric hydra symbol with clean sans-serif typography. The hydra — a multi-headed mythological creature — represents resilience, adaptability, and the branching hydrogen molecule.

Deep navy grounds the identity in technological authority, while teal signals environmental credibility. Together they create a brand that feels both credible to fleet operators and welcoming to everyday riders.

Design Principles

Four principles that shape every screen

Every visual and interaction decision in HydraVoyage maps back to one of four core principles — ensuring the product feels coherent whether you're a first-time passenger or an experienced fleet operator.

🌿

Clean Energy Aesthetic

Gradients from emerald to blue represent hydrogen and sustainability — making clean energy visible rather than invisible.

🔲

Glassmorphism

Frosted glass surfaces with subtle borders and backdrop blur create depth without visual noise — essential for data-dense operator screens.

✦

Glowing Accents

Logo marks and key icons feature soft emerald and blue glows — communicating energy and precision without feeling cold or clinical.

→

Fluid Motion

Smooth transitions and gradient flows suggest forward movement — reinforcing the sense of a journey in progress at every interaction point.

User Flows

Operator & Passenger Journeys

HydraVoyage serves two distinct users with different needs and contexts — fleet operators managing logistics in the field, and passengers experiencing clean transit for the first time. Each flow was designed separately, then connected through shared data infrastructure.

🚛

Operator Flow

Delivered through a tablet or mobile app, operators gain real-time access to fuel availability, vehicle status, and route management.

  • Station Operational Status System — live view of hydrogen station availability along the route
  • Passenger Flow dashboard — capacity, boarding status, and real-time headcounts
  • Passenger insights through voluntary QR code data from the app or physical bus terminal
👤

Passenger Flow

Simple booking with live route tracking and real-time carbon-impact metrics that make clean transit feel tangible — not abstract.

  • Book and pay through the app; ticket viewable and downloadable at any time
  • At boarding, a QR code scan reveals the Boarding Screen with live trip updates
  • In-journey carbon savings displayed in real time throughout the ride
⚡

Fuel Network

An API-driven data layer connects fueling stations, route optimization, and real-time scheduling — the invisible backbone of reliable hydrogen transit.

  • Station status synced to operator dashboard and route planner
  • Predictive alerts when fuel reserves approach route minimums
  • Centralized maintenance and fueling coordination for fleet operators
