Overview

How can Airbnb get more positive reviews?

An end-to-end data analysis and UX project exploring what drives positive reviews on Airbnb. Starting from a public dataset of 30,478 NYC listings, this project combines statistical analysis, competitive benchmarking, and UX redesign proposals to surface actionable improvements for both hosts and the platform.

Methodology & Tools

Data to design, end-to-end

Problem Statement

The review gap

Despite being the world's leading short-term rental platform, Airbnb's review distribution is uneven — certain property types, neighborhoods, and host behaviors consistently underperform. The question: what actually drives a positive review, and how can the platform and its hosts act on that data?

Key Insights

What the data revealed

After regression analysis on 22,152 cleansed NYC listings, five statistically significant patterns emerged.

  • Price has a significant positive correlation with score — for every dollar increase in price, there is a ~0.5% increase in review score.
  • Entire homes and apartments consistently outperform shared and private rooms; dorms average 84.5 and campers/RVs 81 — the two lowest categories.
  • Manhattan properties, especially lofts and homes, are the most expensive across all NYC neighborhoods.
  • Lofts and townhouses maintain consistently high reviews across every neighborhood studied.
  • Brooklyn delivers the highest average review scores and is the second most expensive market — a strong value signal for promotion.

Data Visualization

The dashboard, animated

Each chart from the Tableau analysis — score by room type, score by neighborhood, and price by borough — rebuilt in Remotion and animated frame-by-frame.

Recommendations

Four strategic opportunities

💰

Value Signaling

Use tiered pricing UI that pairs price, expected score, and key highlights — leveraging the price-quality correlation to set accurate expectations.

🏠

Property Prioritization

Elevate entire homes, townhouses, and lofts in search and recommendation — these consistently earn higher scores and drive more bookings.

🗺️

Neighborhood Positioning

Expand Brooklyn inventory and promote it as a premium-value market — high scores at competitive prices make it an ideal spotlight region.

⬆️

Experience Uplift

Provide structured host guidance for dorms and campers — targeted improvement prompts can lift scores and long-term viability of these listing types.

UX Feature Improvements

Property type filtering & score-first design

The analysis directly informed three new UX patterns: a prominent property-type filter surfacing entire homes by default, score badges on listing cards, and a comparison panel letting users pin and evaluate up to three listings side-by-side.
